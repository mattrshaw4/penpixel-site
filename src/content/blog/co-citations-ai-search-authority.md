Backlinks have been SEO’s golden ticket for decades. But a quieter signal is taking center stage with the rise of AI search: **co-citations.**

If your brand isn’t being mentioned alongside the topics you want to own, AI tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity may not recognize you… even if your site is strong.

## What Exactly Is a Co-Citation?
First explained to me by [James Allen, a prolific author for Search Engine Land](https://searchengineland.com/author/james-allen) and Senior Technical SEO & Analytics Specialist, a co-citation occurs when your **brand name appears in proximity to key industry terms**, even without a hyperlink.

Examples:

- A Gartner or Forrester report that mentions your brand in a list of category players.
- A Reddit thread where your name appears in the same conversation as “kitchen remodeling in *[location]*”
- An industry article where you’re cited alongside competitors in the same vertical.

To AI tools, these associations act like connective tissue: *“This brand is relevant to this topic.”*

## Why Co-Citations Matter More Than Backlinks
AI doesn’t just scan for blue hyperlinks. It [maps relationships between brands](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/how-search-works), concepts, and authority sources, which is really just one piece of the [broader contextual signal framework](/blog/contextual-signals-ai-search-authority) AI uses to weigh who's trustworthy.

That means:

- **Mentions can matter more than links.** A citation in a respected context boosts credibility, regardless of whether there is a link or not.
- **Context drives authority.** Being named next to your competitors signals parity and helps AI classify you.
- **Breadth beats volume.** A few high-quality mentions across trusted platforms outweigh dozens of generic backlinks.

## Where to Build Co-Citations
1. **Industry Reports & Analyst Mentions:** Get your brand into comparison reports, roundups, or analyst commentary. These are AI goldmines.
2. **Peer-Driven Platforms:** Participate in conversations on LinkedIn, Reddit, or Quora. Natural mentions here are scraped frequently by AI tools.
3. **Third-Party Articles & Listicles:** Earn mentions in “Top 10” lists or thought-leadership roundups. Even if unlinked, these boost your contextual footprint.
4. **Case Studies & Press Releases:** Ensure that your partners, clients, or publications mention [your name and target terms in conjunction](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/earned-media).

## How to Encourage Co-Citations Without Forcing Them
- **Create reference-able assets.** Publish benchmarks, research, or tools that others will cite naturally.
- **Engage in industry conversations.** Drop your perspective into ongoing debates where your name becomes tied to the topic.
- **Encourage partners to mention you.** In joint announcements, ensure that your brand and expertise keywords appear together.
- **Seed FAQs and summaries.** Structured answers on your site provide journalists and analysts with easy-to-reuse phrasing.

## Your Next Question (Answered)
**“Do I still need backlinks if co-citations matter so much?”**

Yes — but the weighting has shifted. Backlinks still play a role in Google rankings, but AI-driven search considers the broader context. A mix of **strong links + strategic co-citations** is the new authority play.
